Subject: You're Using AI. But Are You Type A or B?

Two engineers. Same tools. One ships features. The other ships faster keystrokes.

The difference isn't the software — it's the paradigm. Type A tells AI *how* to implement something and stays in the driver's seat of every line. Type B describes *what* problem needs solving, dispatches it to an agentic workflow, then reviews and judges the output. One is a faster craftsperson. The other is the PM of a small agent team.

That distinction explains why 75% of engineers use AI tools, yet most organizations see no measurable delivery gains. Type A adds AI to an existing workflow. The bottlenecks — requirements clarity, review quality, architectural judgment — stay exactly where they were.

The shift is smaller than it sounds. Before your next ticket: write one paragraph describing the problem. Hand that to your AI system before you touch a single line of code. That's the starting posture.
