Subject: When the API Bill Gets Too Loud to Ignore

I've been talking to engineering leaders watching their AI spend spiral — thousands of euros a month on OpenAI, Google, and Claude APIs, processing millions of tokens per minute at peak. The costs are crushing.

A well-planned self-hosted GPU migration can project around 60% cost reduction, full operational control, and — here's the surprise — 18-25% lower energy draw per processed document. That's tens of grams of CO₂e saved per 1,000 tokens.

The lesson? Infrastructure decisions aren't just about cost anymore. They sit at the intersection of cost, control, and carbon. When you show engineers "saving €X also saved Y kWh," the conversation shifts from procurement to purpose.
