<!-- buttondown-editor-mode: plaintext --># Bear Essentials Newsletter: Deployment Brigade System

**Subject:** Stop Serving Month-Old Food

---

Monthly deployments are like serving **30-day-old food**. When something's wrong, you can't isolate which dish is the problem—you're throwing out the entire buffet.

A few years back, my team was stuck in deployment hell. **17 engineers, three-hour war rooms, monthly releases.** Nobody owned anything. Deployments were events, not routine.

The breakthrough came from *The Bear*—yes, the FX show. When we stopped treating deployments as crisis events and started treating them like **dinner service**, everything changed.

**Three principles:**

- **Reduce batch size** (1-2 changes per day, not 30)
- **Clear ownership** (the test-env owner locks the environment—not a committee)
- **Service-level accountability** (stop saying "deployment," start saying "service")

Within weeks: **Daily deploys. Zero drama.** Rollbacks became routine, not crisis. The team never asks "when will we deploy?" Service runs on schedule.

**This week:** Ask your team, "What would need to be true for us to deploy today?" Write down every excuse. Pick the biggest blocker. Fix it.
