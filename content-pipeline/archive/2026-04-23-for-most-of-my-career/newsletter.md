Subject: Stop Staging.

For most of my career I confused two things: open-sourcing my own side projects, and contributing to other people's. Fifteen years of unshipped repos in `~/hack/` later, I figured out they are not the same. And that reading CONTRIBUTING.md over and over is the dev equivalent of staging at someone else's kitchen.

The trap is the cultural script. Every "first contribution" guide points you at someone else's CONTRIBUTING.md: forty lines of branch-naming rules, edge-case checklists, a maintainer's testy response to the last PR. None of it is wrong. But it turns drive-by contribution into apprenticeship, and most evenings I do not have apprenticeship in me.

The unlock (whatever label you want to put on it: imposter syndrome, perfectionism, permission-seeking) was realizing I could just start my own repo. With Claude Code the polish-tax that used to kill side projects (README, Homebrew tap, release Action, social card) collapses into the same evening as the prototype.

This week: if you have a private repo you keep private out of polish-perfectionism rather than genuine doubt, ship the one you are least embarrassed about.
